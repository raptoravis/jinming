Confidence Rating
Confidence Rating
Confidence Rating
Shaw’s new Confidence Rating System assesses three separate carpet characteristics: durability, ease of maintenance, and softness.

Durability – The durability rating reflects a carpet’s expected durability and appearance retention capability. Rather than being based only on wear testing, however, durability evaluates the carpet construction in addition to hexapod or contract walker testing.

Ease of Maintenance – The ease of maintenance rating represents the level of maintenance required to keep a carpet looking its best – the higher the rating, the easier the carpet is to clean and maintain. Factors related to ease of maintenance include fiber type, construction, and stain/soil treatment. 

Softness – Softness is primarily determined by fiber type and the thickness of the individual fibers. Heavier, denser carpets feel more luxurious and comfortable.

Face Weight
Face Weight
Face weight is measured as per unit area of pile yarn floor coverings according to ASTM (American Society for Testing Materials) method D-5848. Due to several processes involved in manufacturing, such as shearing, the actual finished weight may reflect a variance of up to 7%. The weight is determined by compensating for the factor of commercial moisture regain, as defined in ASTM D-1909. The ASTM measurements are accepted industry norms and the most accurate means of determining face weight.
